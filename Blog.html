    Programming and Anxiety

    Mental illness plays a very big role in our lives with 45% of all Australians
    and one in four people world wide who suffer from a mental illness.
    With saying that… When i was fresh out of high school i was diagnosed with mild anxiety
    and i had been getting headaches for most of my life (well from what i can remember anyway).
    I was also diagnosed with a small learning disease, I found it hard to keep up with high school
    and with that i was told that it’d be better if i left school and go to Tafe.
    As i grew up i no longer saw my learning difficulty as a wall that i couldn’t get past
    but i started seeing it as a bully and that i needed to step up to this bully and tell it to go away.
    So that’s exactly what i did, whenever i would start a new job or field of study
    I would set out to prove to myself and sometimes others that didn’t think i could do something that even though
    I had so much more to do then others because i had to find how i learnt things best and sometimes that meant
    that I wouldn’t pass certain things because i would try to do things by myself and not ask for help.
    Headaches have been with me since i could remember, they’ve impacted my life quite bad with them ranging from (okay, i can handle this) to (f#ck this!),
    They’ve been a constant annoyance and they’ve made me miss out on so much.
    Last week was R U Okay? day and we had some people from class share about their struggles which was awesome!
    and it was great to see so many people care and cheer for the people who did share about what they are or have been through.
    I sometimes wish that it was always like that, and always that easy to be able to come out
    and tell people that no you may not be okay some days and that mental illness of any kind
    whether it be as little as headaches to as big as depression and suicidal thoughts,
    there’s such a big concept that people should just suck it up and get over there struggles and that is not okay. People should be allowed to say what’s on their mind and be told “Thank you for sharing” or be told that people do care about your struggles and that you’re not alone. This week in class we’re working on our portfolio assessments where we have to create a Portfolio from what we’ve learnt so far from html and css
    and then upload it to the interwebs for people to see and then present it to the class.
    Continue to follow me as i transform
    from being a guy with no coding experience to a full-stack web developer
    in just 6 months.
